Why this programme?

Thinking about Headship is a professional learning programme being planned for delivery by the West Partnership as part of their growing programme for staff from across our eight partner local authorities. The change in policy requiring all prospective headteachers to have the necessary training and a commitment to career long professional learning places this course as part of the journey towards headship.

The programme is designed to act as a preparatory step on the journey to headship for participants- particularly with the mandatory completion of the Standard for Headship for all new headteachers in Scotland which came into action in August 2020.

This course is not yet a formal qualification but will provide a learning experience which offers those taking part the chance to take steps to prepare themselves for the next stage in their senior leadership career.

The experience of learning alongside colleagues from across the West Partnership area will create an opportunity for connections to be made and for deeper learning conversation to take place.

The programme is structured around three broad themes linked directly to the revised Standards for Headship:

  • Professional Values, Commitment and Standard for Headship
  • Professional Knowledge and Understanding
  • Professional Skills and Abilities

The course aims to develop a deeper understanding of effective headship and strategic leadership as well as introducing participants to the Masters Level programme ‘Into Headship’ offered by Education Scotland and in partnership with a number of universities, which allows completion of the mandatory Standard for Headship qualification.

Elements of the West Partnership - Thinking About Headship course will include:

  • Reflection on the Standards
  • The policy landscape
  • Leading and managing strategic change

It is anticipated that participants will also undertake a strategic task within their establishment with a focus on an area linked to their School Improvement Plan/current context. This will be presented to the participant group towards the end of the programme’s duration.

At present the programme would be delivered virtually and as twilight sessions for all participants.

Next Levels of Study

Mhis programme of study is designed as a precursor to study to the Masters level programme Into Headship.

Entry Requirements

  • Minimum of 2 years of senior leadership experience or equivalent
  • Evidence of relevant professional learning linked to improved outcomes for children and young people
  • Evidence of having successfully led a range of whole school initiatives linked to improved outcomes for children and young people

How to Apply

Interested applicants must be endorsed by their Headteacher and Local Authority and must complete the attached application form to be returned via their Local Authority contact to West Partnership Lead Officer, Jen Crocket at jcrocket@boclair.e-dunbarton.sch.uk no later than Monday 30th November 2020.

Any questions about the programme should also be directed to Jen Crocket at the email address above.
