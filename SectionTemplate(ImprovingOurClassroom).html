Improving Our Classroom

Improving Our Classrooms (IOC) is a visionary Glasgow City Council Education Services initiative which responded to the needs of its teachers and to Teaching: Scotland’s Future. In September 2011, Glasgow City Council Education Services, in conjunction with ASPECT took the lead in Scotland in piloting CLPL that provided an intensive course, which encouraged depth of learning over an extended period for Primary Class Teachers. Improving Our Classrooms recognised that teacher quality is the key to meeting the needs of learners in the 21st Century securing the ambition of Curriculum for Excellence.

Improving Our Classrooms provides a programme of high-quality professional learning, placing classroom practitioners at the heart of school improvement. It has a clear focus on high quality self-evaluation at classroom level leading to improved learning and teaching, data informed targeted interventions, improved attainment and achievement with a relentless focus on equity and excellence for all children and young people.

IOC Digital Model

In 2020/2021 a digital model of delivery for Improving Our Classrooms was developed and delivered as a pilot in Glasgow and four other local authorities in the West Partnership Regional Improvement Collaborative (South Lanarkshire, East Dunbartonshire, East Renfrewshire and Inverclyde).

In this model the course content is delivered via twilight webinars and pre/post webinar discussions via Microsoft Teams and teachers engage with an IOC Learning Journal throughout the course to facilitate professional reading, self-evaluation and collaboration. Sessions are designed explicitly to develop and apply the understanding that teachers need to identify the most important aspects which they can change to promote improvement in their class and to review their impact.

Teachers form Trios to encourage self and peer evaluation and collaboration throughout the course with a clear focus on improvements to the success and achievements of their learners. All Trios of teachers are matched with a Mentor who will guide and support them through the improvement process.

The IOC course content includes the following elements:

  • The Big Picture: International, National and Local Research and Policy
  • The Refreshed Curriculum Narrative
  • Data Informed Self Evaluation at Classroom Level and Evaluative Writing
  • Learning Intentions and Success Criteria
  • Effective Questioning, Active Learning, Reviewing and Connecting the Learning
  • Feedback, Dialogue and Learner Conversations
  • Differentiation, Pace and Challenge
  • Academic Skills (with Glasgow Caledonian University)
  • The Creative Classroom
  • Metacognition
  • Examples of Case Studies of Improvement
  • Assessment and Moderation
  • Parental Engagement, Involvement and Family Learning
  • Digital tools to enhance key areas of learning and teaching (with Glasgow Digital Leaders of Learning)

Mentors

Teachers submit a five thousand word Case Study of Improvement at the end of Improving Our Classrooms and, if successful, are awarded GTCS Accreditation and 30 Master Level Credits from Glasgow City Council which are credit rated by Caledonian University.

In order to further support teachers with data informed self-evaluation leading to their Case Study of Improvement, Senior Leaders across the West Partnership Regional Improvement Collaborative are offered opportunities to be involved in an IOC Mentoring Role. This has been designed in partnership with Glasgow Caledonian University and will lead to mentors being awarded 15 Masters Level Credits. Assessment of this will be through a structured professional dialogue with an IOC tutor based on a reflective journal which will reflect on their journey as a mentor.

Whole School Model

A Whole School Model of IOC is currently being developed, which will be facilitated by identified Primary Senior Leaders under the guidance of West Partnership IOC Leads. There will be guidance based on Implementation Science to assist with a school’s readiness to engage in this model and teachers in identified schools will be given the opportunity to gain accreditation if they complete a Case Study of Improvement. There will be one school from each Local Authority identified in April 2021 as part of the Pilot. A Senior Leader will be identified and trained to lead this model of school improvement based on a journal approach. The pilot of this model will begin in August 2021 for the five identified schools.
