    Performance, Planning & Research

    The PPR team provides support to educational establishments and headquarters staff around all aspects of performance and planning information. This includes the collection, submission and dissemination of information for the purposes of statutory returns to the Scottish Government and also the extraction and analysis of data from Education Services MIS systems for the purposes of performance monitoring and reporting. We also contribute to the development and monitoring of Education Service Plans and the Council Strategic Plan

SEEMiS

SEEMIS is the Education Management Information System (MIS) provider for all 32 local authorities and schools across Scotland. It is a critical platform that provides essential administrative functionality to all users within the school education sector in Scotland.

Additionally, SEEMIS is used to support key education stakeholder and agencies such as SQA, ScotXed (data exchange to support census returns), SNSA and Glow.

SEEMiS

SEEMiS no longer accept technical support requests via telephone and have now moved to a Community Self Service Helpdesk process. This will mean that if you require to report a fault you must log a Helpdesk call via the Community Self Service. To enable users to do this they must now set up a Community Portal account using the guidance notes available on the link below.

SEEMiS Next Generation (SEEMIS Early Years & SEEMIS Schools)

Following a strategic review of the current SEEMIS system it was assessed as reaching end of life and no longer fit for purpose in the digital age.

Subsequently, a project is now under way with a co-development partner (Alscient) to develop SEEMIS Next Generation. This is being funded by all 32 Local Authorities through the annual payments made to SEEMIS. At the same time an Early Years MIS is being developed to replace NAMS (funded by the Scottish Government).

Please see the links below for regular updates on the progress of the rollout to both SEEMIS Early Years and SEEMIS Schools.

Glasgow MIS (Early Years Pastoral Notes and Early Years Wellbeing)

There is no pastoral aspect to the new SEEMIS Early Years system, however, in partnership with colleagues in Gateway Shared Services, Glasgow have developed an Early Years Pastoral Notes system (launched in October 2020) to support early year’s colleagues in local authority establishments and are currently developing an Early Years Wellbeing Application (launch due August 2021)

Please see the links below for regular updates on the progress of the rollout of the new Early Years applications.
