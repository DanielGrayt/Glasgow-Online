Director of Education Services

Douglas Hutchison
Executive director of education, Glasgow City Council
25/02/2022
  • Directorate

He’s been in the new executive director seat for just over six weeks now, so we thought it was time to sit down with Douglas Hutchison and find out a bit about him – his background, his plans and aspirations for learning and teaching in Glasgow…and how he has been out and about visiting schools and settling into HQ.

Our video interview covers Douglas’ employment record to date, a bit about his family and how he sees his tenure developing after taking over from Maureen at the start of the year.

We were able to film him at one of the headteacher’s meetings in the City Chambers and then out on a drop-in visit to St Roch’s Primary school to meet pupils and staff.

It is Douglas’ plan to get out and about as much as possible to schools and nurseries and to chat to staff to find out their thoughts.

So, meet the man in charge of learning and teaching in the city…

Glasgow is a very attractive prospect, for a variety of reasons. Glasgow in terms of education is a big success story.

It’s attractive building on Maureen’s legacy, building on what’s been done here. But there is a great opportunity to transform the lives of so many children and young people.

I have been hugely impressed with the way that schools and young people have coped over the last two years of the pandemic. I am really keen to be out in schools and get a sense of what is going on and that has been really good so far and to meet children who were really positive about their learning.”
