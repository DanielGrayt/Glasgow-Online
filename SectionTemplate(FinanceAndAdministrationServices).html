Finance and Administration Services

Corporate Finance (Education) is responsible for providing a comprehensive financial service to Education Services.

The team is also responsible for delivering a full management accounting service and the collation and completion of financial accounts.  Corporate Finance has a key role in supporting the council's service reform agenda by providing detailed financial planning advice and support for all of the major development projects.

Areas include:-

  • Early Years
  • Primary
  • Secondary
  • ASL
  • Other

Our key priorities include:-

  • Constructing the details of Education Services budget
  • Processing ongoing adjustments including budget realignments
  • Processing journals to correct the allocation of actual expenditure
  • 4 weekly Financial Monitoring of Educations Services budget
  • Liaison with colleagues in Education & Financial Services to provide regular monitoring reports to Education Committee
  • Inter Departmental Transactions (IDTs)
  • Preparation of the Education Services Yearly Probable Outturn, and Final Accounts
  • Provide advice and guidance on financial management

[Glossary description]

Name Description
Budget The monetary value set aside for a particular type of expenditure or income
Commitment When schools raise an order on PECOS, it values that order as a commitment against their budget. This does not appear in the financial ledger until the goods/services are received. This allows schools to know how much budget they have left to spend.
Actual Actual expenditure occurs when charges are recorded in the Council’s financial ledger. For example, when the goods/services are receipted on PECOS, it changes the charge from commitment to actual.
Accrual At the end of the financial year, we seek to ensure that all charges relevant to that financial year are included. If the actual expenditure has not been recorded in the ledger, we can ‘accrue’ the expenditure to make sure it is in the correct financial year.
Variance We call the difference between budget and actual income or expenditure the variance, or difference.
Monitoring The process of comparing budget to actual, which is undertaken every 4-week period throughout the financial year, is known as Monitoring. A monitoring report is submitted to the Council to let them see how Education is performing against their budgets.
SAP This is the Council’s data processing system which records all Financial and HR transactions and information
Business Warehouse (BW) This is the Council’s reporting tool used to produce reports from information held in SAP
PECOS This is an electronic procurement system used by Scottish Local Authorities to purchase goods and services
PULSE This is the system Services use to manage requests/workload for any information or actions required from Customer & Business Services (CBS)
JOURNAL – Actual & Budget Actual – This is the process to rectify any miscoding’s, errors or Departmental recharges within SAP

Budget – This is the process to download budgets to schools/establishments and other Departments within SAP or make amendments to existing budget/phasing.
Per Capita This is the core budget used to purchase resources which is allocated to schools/establishments based on the number of pupils on roll.
Devolved School Management (DSM) Education Services operates a scheme of Devolved School Management (DSM). This scheme is designed to give schools a significant level of control over their own budgets whilst at the same time affording them the safeguards associated with being part of a large education authority. Of the total budget set aside for Primary and Secondary direct service delivery, approximately 90% of this is devolved to schools. (includes an allocation of Central Support Services)
School Fund Many Educational establishments operate a School Fund to collect money for the School, outwith the Council’s ledger, this money can be used to enhance the facilities and equipment available to their establishment.
Financial Year This is the accounting period which begins 1st April and end 31st March. Each financial year has 13 periods with each period covering 4 weeks.
Monitoring The process of comparing budget to actual, which is undertaken every 4-week period throughout the financial year, is known as Monitoring. A monitoring report is submitted to the Council to let them see how Education is performing against their budgets.
Authorised Signatory Glasgow City Council requires a list of staff signatories who can authorise and approve spend, as well as signing invoices. Only those staff with this approval can process these payments.
Scheme of Delegation This is the Council document which governs the management of devolved school budgets. Head Teachers must refer to this when they have responsibility of budgets.
Virement This is the transfer of funds from one budget heading/area/department to another
Revenue Revenue is spending on operational costs such as Staffing, Resources etc.
Capital Capital investment is spending on property and other assets that the Council will use over many years to provide public services.
Admin & Finance Assistant (AFA) Each school is allocated an AFA who is managed by Customer & Business Services (CBS) to assist the Head Teacher with their allocated budget. They provided advice and guidance to the school as well as monthly reports on expenditure to date. Go to the School Directory to find out who the AFA for your school is.

[FAQ description]

Question Answer
How can I find what budget is allocated to a school? Education Services Financial & Compliance team issue the Head Teacher and School Board Chairperson of each school, a budget report at 1st April each year, setting out the budget allocated to the school for the coming financial year. These statements are also available online #####LINK####. Each year the Scottish Government publishes comparative figures for budgets allocated to all schools in Scotland, which allows comparison of the budgets across Councils.
What control do schools have in terms of this budget? The budget allocated is devolved to the Head Teacher of the school, who is governed by a Council document known as the Scheme of Delegation. This document sets out the rules concerning the ability of the head Teacher to request that budgets can be moved from one budget line to another, to properly target the needs of the school. This process is known as virement, and the Scheme of Delegation sets out the availability of, and limits to, the amount of virement applicable to each budget line.
As a responsible member of staff, how do I alert the Authority of a suspected theft in my workplace? In the first instance, it is recommended that you alert your line manager to the circumstances which have aroused your suspicions, or which you know as fact. The manager will then be responsible for altering the Authority following the guidelines set out in Management Circular 44, which is available on the Go Glasgow website ###LINK###
I have been asked to look after the School Fund for my school. How can I be sure I am doing this correctly? Although School Funds are deemed not to be part of the Council’s official funds, Education Services, in conjunction with the Council’s Internal Audit section, have prepared Management Circular 23###LINK### which sets out the procedures governing the recording of School Funds. Staff are therefore, encouraged to familiarise themselves with the contents of this Circular. Further guidance and help can be offered by Education Financial Management & Compliance staff.
I require clarification about the correct code to be used for a certain item of expenditure. Who do I contact to obtain this clarification? Contact your Admin & Financial Assistant (AFA) Glasgow Online LINK### with details of the charge and the proper codes will be determined.
When do Internal Audit become involved with the activities of Education Services? Internal Audit undertake a series of planned audits throughout the Council, including Education Services. The audit programme as it applies to Education is agreed with the Education Head of Service Development prior to commencement to each audit. Additionally, Internal Audit can be called upon to either undertake ad-hoc audits to examine areas identified by Education Services, or simply to offer advice to Education Staff on matters which may cause concern.
